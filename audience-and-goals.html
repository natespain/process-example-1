Audience and Goals

After researching different aspects around A Candle in the Window, I developed goals for each of the audiences I identified earlier.

Audience

To define our Audience, we did an exercise where we brainstormed all the possible users of the website and then organized them into user groups. This would help us organize people groups and determine goals later on.

This is the product of my brainstorm. My user groups were Organizations, Nonmembers/possible members, and Members.

  1. Organizations included those who would advertise and promote, Donors, and Skeptics. These could all possibly overlap but I must provide solutions for each need to be as extensive as possible.
  2. Nonmembers and possible members included tired missionaries, general travelers, and students. Missionaries often get burned out on the field from not taking sabbatical. ACW aims to help stop this by giving them low cost get-away options to enjoy fellowship and be refreshed. General travelers could be families, or Christian adults looking to travel and have fellowship. Students may be looking for cheap and safe travel where they can save money and have enriched experiences.
  3. Members include big families with kids, missionary’s and pastor’s families specifically (due to their free membership), couples, members who host, and retired missionaries. All of these groups are global and diverse and have different backgrounds which plays into the goals and usability.

To ensure that we meet each user need, we developed overarching goals fir the client and audience as well as goals the website should meet.

Goals

Overarching Goals

Client
  • Gain new members
  • Connect travelers (who and where)
  • Connect to user stories on Instagram, the blog, and Facebook
  • Encourage Christian Hospitality
Audience
  • Members
    • Book accomodations quickly and safely
    • Communicate to host easily
    • Save money
    • Enjoy fellowship of global community
    • Missionary sabbatical
    • Renew Membership
  • Nonmembers
    • Gather information about A Candle in the Window
    • Trust and join
    • Donate money
Website
  • Get new members who trust
  • Quickly move members through hosting/booking communication
  • Keep members
  • Share stories

Specific Goals

User Goal Content/Action
Members
  • Connectivity with other Christian families
  • Travel
  • Share their experience
  • Login to book a stay
  • Host a stay
  • Make a donation
  • Share a story
  • Read the blog
  • Book easily on their phone or tablet
Interested Non-members
  • Learn more
  • Read about experiences
  • Explore possibilities
  • Join the network
  • Blog with stories
  • Read about different memberships
  • Call to action to join ACW
  • Option to share it with their friends
Organizations/Bloggers/Advertisers
  • Support ACW/donate
  • Advertise ACW on their site/blog
  • Badge to put on their blog
  • Stories of user experience
  • "Share" button
Donors
  • Find out the worthiness of the organization
  • See where their money goes
  • Find their “why?”,
  • Gain trust
  • Successful stories of user experience
  • Action to donate with small information about where money goes
  • "About" page describing who runs the organization.

Goals Summary

I used both the primary and secondary research as well as the interviews to determine the needs and goals for the website. Creating these goals centered around people sets the website up to meet user and client needs and create an easy user experience.
