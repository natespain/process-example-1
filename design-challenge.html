Design Challenge

Defining the Design Challenge was the first step. I used the IDEO Human Centered Design Toolkit as a guide to posing questions to find the root problems of the website for users.

What is the problem you're trying to solve?

Getting people to join A Candle in the Window’s network hosting travelers and staying at host’s houses.

The Design Question:

How does A Candle in the Window website help users join the network to make faith-based connections and navigate hosting and requesting with ease?

The Ultimate Impact

The goal is to grow the network of members and the frequency of which they use A Candle in the Window.

Possible Solutions

Simplifing the homepage and fonts, creating hierarchy, using photography, and clarifying the call to action as well as using negative space could all help solve the problems.

Context and Constraints

The website must be on a subscription based networking site, global users and their needs, and not up-to-date information.

The Design Question Restated

"How can the re-design of this website attract new global members and simplify the hosting/booking process?"

This excercise got me thinking on person-based design terms. By identifying the roots of the problems and desired outcome, I was able to map out how to take steps towards the solution.
